■ Turmeric (turmeric)
・ Put pink and yellow flowers.
Zingiberaceae
・ Dried roots are used for flavoring and coloring.
・ Promotes blood circulation, treats bruises, suppresses blood coagulation, reduces toxins in the liver, and helps fat metabolism.
・ In India, it is said to be good for skin care.
・ In Japan, it is said to be good for purifying the liver and preventing cancer.
Applications: Curry powder, pickles, textile dyes, etc.

■ Cardamom (cardamom)
・ It has white flowers with purple streaks and bears fragrant green fruits. Zingiberaceae
・ It is called the King of Spices.
・ It is an expensive spice, and when you chew the seeds, it gives you a sweet scent.
-It has digestive, stimulating and sedative effects, roots are used for fever reduction, and fruit essential oils are used for liqueurs and perfumes.
Uses: Garam masala, curry powder, chai, coffee, pastries, etc.

■ Chile (pepper)
・ It is an annual or perennial plant and is complicated in terms of plant classification. Solanaceae
・ It is rich in vitamin C and has a digestive promoting effect.
・ Capsaicin, a pungent ingredient, promotes blood circulation and excites sensory nerves.
・ Sterilizes food and relieves sore throat.
Uses: Salads, pickles, spicy curry, etc.

■ Cumin (cumin)
・ It is an annual plant with white or pink flowers that bloom in summer, and produces fragrant nuts covered with fine thorns. Umbelliferae
・ Seeds and seeds help digestion and reduce intestinal gas and diarrhea.
・ Has tonic and stimulating effects.
・ Essential oil can also be used as a massage oil to remove fat.
Uses: Mixed spices, pickles, liqueurs, perfumes, etc.

■ Fenugreek (Koroha)
-The leaves are egg-shaped trefoil, and cream-colored flowers bloom in summer. Leguminosae
・ When the seeds are soaked in water, it becomes a lotion that softens the skin, and becomes a lip balm or a massage oil for the scalp.
・ Seed tea relieves tonics and digestive pain and lowers fever.
・ Reduces blood cholesterol and urinary sugar in adult diabetics.
Uses: Curry powder, chutney, salad, etc.

■ Mustard (mustard)
・ It is an annual plant and yellow flowers bloom in spring. Brassicaceae
・ Crushed seeds feel pungent when mixed with cold water and the enzyme is activated.
・ It has a blood circulation promoting effect and is used for the treatment of bronchitis and foot bath.
Uses: Salad dressings, soups, meat dishes, etc.

■ Pepper (pepper)
・ It is a perennial vine with white flowers and fruits that turn from green to deep red. Piperaceae
・ It is said to be the king of spices and has black, green and white pepper.
-The alkaloid piperine contained in pepper promotes saliva and gastric juice, helps digestion and sterilization, and is said to be good for the skin in India.
Uses: Pickles, salads, general dishes, etc.

■ Coriander (coriander)
・ It is an annual plant and has a unique fragrance. Umbelliferae
・ The leaves have a mild anesthetic effect, and the seeds have a mild sedative effect.
・ Helps digestion, reduces intestinal gas, and relieves migraines.
Applications: Pickles, curries, liqueurs, perfumes, etc.

Saffron (saffron)
・ Mauve flowers with three vermilion stigmas protruding in autumn. Iridaceae
・ Since saffron is only stigma and pistil, more than 1700 flowers are required to make 25g of dried saffron. Therefore, it is said to be the most expensive golden spice.
・ It is effective for calming nerves, bruising, rheumatic pain, and neuralgia.
Uses: Makeup and sacred dyes. Bouillabaisse, paella, etc.

■ Clove (clove)
・ The cream-colored flowers of evergreens turn red when the stamens fall, and produce purple fruits. Myrtaceae
・ Add flavor to plants and prevent vehicle sickness.
・ Flowers relieve eye pain.
・ Clove oil is used as a flavoring agent, pesticide, and pain reliever.
Use: For flavoring dishes, etc.

Cinnamon (Ceylon Nikkei)
・ It is an evergreen tree with cream-colored flowers and purple fruits. Lauraceae
・ Bark adds flavor to dishes and becomes a fragrance.
・ Essential oil has bactericidal and tonic effects and is used for the treatment of nausea and hypertension.
Uses: Incense of sweets and beverages, curry, etc.
